My Favorite Authors


Fëdor Dostoevsky

Fëdor Dostoevskij (1821–1881) was a Russian novelist, short story writer, essayist, and philosopher. He is considered one of the greatest writers in world literature. Born in Moscow, Dostoevsky faced numerous hardships throughout his life, including a death sentence (which was later commuted to exile in Siberia) and a lifelong struggle with epilepsy and gambling addiction. His works are famous for their deep exploration of psychology, morality, and existentialism. Themes of suffering, the nature of good and evil, and the complexities of human freedom are central to his novels. Some of his most renowned works include Crime and Punishment, The Brothers Karamazov, and The Idiot, which delve into the moral and spiritual struggles of the human soul.

My favorite book: Crime and Punishment



Agatha Christie

Agatha Christie (1890–1976) was an English writer known for her 66 detective novels, 14 short story collections, and 17 plays. She is often called the "Queen of Crime" for her contribution to the mystery genre. Christie’s most famous detectives are Hercule Poirot and Miss Marple, who have become iconic in the world of crime fiction. Her novels, with intricate plots and surprising twists, have captivated readers for decades. Born in Torquay, England, Christie led a varied life, including a period during World War II working in a hospital and later publishing her autobiography. Her works have been translated into over 100 languages, and she is one of the best-selling authors of all time. Her legacy in the mystery genre continues to influence writers and filmmakers today.

My favorite book: And Then There Were None



Yuval Noah Harari

Yuval Noah Harari is an Israeli historian, professor, and author, best known for his thought-provoking books that tackle large-scale questions about humanity’s past, present, and future. Born in 1976 in Haifa, Israel, Harari earned his Ph.D. in history from the University of Oxford and specializes in world history, macro-historical processes, and the human condition. He gained international fame with his book Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind, which explores the history of Homo sapiens from the Stone Age to modern times, examining how we became the dominant species on Earth. His subsequent works, including Homo Deus: A Brief History of Tomorrow and 21 Lessons for the 21st Century, delve into the future of humanity in the age of artificial intelligence and biotechnology. Harari's books challenge readers to reflect on the impact of technology and human choices on the future of society and the planet.

My favorite book: Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind
