Fiction

Fiction books tell imagined stories that explore emotions, adventures, and fantastical worlds.

Romance Fantasy Sci-Fi Dystopian Adventure Mystery Horror Thriller Historical

Non-Fiction

Non-fiction books are based on real people, facts, and events, offering knowledge, insight, or inspiration.

Biography Art Science & Technology Self-Help Humanities & Social Sciences

Romance

Romance novels focus on relationships and emotional connections, often centered around love stories. These books explore passion, heartbreak, and the journey of characters finding or losing love.

Pride and Prejudice by Jane Austen

Fantasy

Fantasy stories take place in imaginary worlds filled with magic, mythical creatures, and supernatural elements. They often feature epic quests, chosen heroes, and rich world-building.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone by J.K. Rowling

Sci-Fi

Science fiction explores futuristic technology, space travel, time manipulation, or alien life. It raises questions about humanity, ethics, and the possible directions of scientific advancement.

Dune by Frank Herbert

Dystopian

Dystopian books are set in dark, often oppressive futures where society has gone wrong. They explore themes like control, rebellion, freedom, and survival in broken systems.

1984 by George Orwell

Adventure

Adventure books are filled with excitement, danger, and action. The protagonist usually faces challenges in unfamiliar places, making for thrilling and fast-paced storytelling.

The Hobbit by J.R.R. Tolkien

Mystery

Mystery novels revolve around solving a puzzle—usually a crime, like a murder or theft. The plot follows clues, red herrings, and clever deductions leading to a final reveal.

Murder on the Orient Express by Agatha Christie

Horror

Horror stories aim to scare, disturb, or unsettle the reader. They involve fear, the supernatural, monsters, or psychological tension, playing on our deepest anxieties.

The Shining by Stephen King

Thriller

Thrillers are intense and fast-moving, full of suspense and unexpected twists. These books keep readers on edge as characters face danger, deception, or high-stakes situations.

The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo by Stieg Larsson

Historical

Historical fiction is set in the past, often with real historical events or figures as the backdrop. It brings different time periods to life through the eyes of fictional characters.

All the Light We Cannot See by Anthony Doerr

Biography

Biographies tell the life stories of real people—famous or not. They provide insight into achievements, struggles, and the human experiences behind a person’s journey.

The Diary of a Young Girl by Anne Frank

Art

Books about art explore creative expression, techniques, and the stories behind artworks and artists. They can be both visual and reflective, covering various forms and movements.

The Story of Art by E.H. Gombrich

Science & Technology

This genre covers discoveries, inventions, and how science and tech shape our world. Books can range from beginner-friendly explanations to deep dives into complex topics.

A Brief History of Time by Stephen Hawking

Self-Help

Self-help books aim to inspire personal growth, offering advice on habits, mindset, relationships, or productivity. They encourage readers to improve aspects of their lives.

The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People by Stephen R. Covey

Humanities & Social Sciences

These books explore human culture, behavior, society, and thought. Covering subjects like philosophy, history, sociology, or politics, they seek to explain how people and societies function.

Sapiens by Yuval Noah Harari
